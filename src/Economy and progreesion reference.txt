# Firebreak Master Framework

## Mission Loop

1. Choose mission
2. Review terrain and weather
3. Start mission
4. Deploy assets during mission
5. Complete mission and earn money and achievements
6. Repair, refuel, restock, and recover
7. Unlock upgrades and tougher missions

---

# Core Economy Rule

* **Money is never spent during missions**
* Money is only used **before and after missions**
* Missions are replayable for money
* Harder missions pay more
* **Fallback government funding** helps prevent failure spirals

---

## Food

Food represents **crew readiness / operator strain**, not just meals.

Used for:

* maintaining crew fed status between missions
* restoring readiness for personnel-operated assets
* Fire Crew usage
* Fire Watch usage
* Drone Recon usage

Rules:

* Food is primarily a **wear-and-recovery system**, not a standard in-mission spend like Fuel
* crew-operated assets generate **Food wear**
* Food wear is resolved between missions through the fed-status system
* crew does **not** become unavailable during a mission from food depletion
* fed status affects the **next mission**
* storage upgraded through **Crew Facilities**

Fed status effects:

* **76–100** = normal cooldown
* **51–75** = cooldown +1
* **26–50** = cooldown +3
* **1–25** = cooldown +5
* **0** = unavailable

* repairing asset durability between missions
* upgrade requirements
* occasional field repair/support needs

Rules:

* purchased with money
* capped by storage
* storage upgraded through **Vehicle Bay**

Repair rule:

* **Repairs cost Parts only**
* **1 Part = 10 durability restored**
* repairs can be **partial between missions**

# Building-Based Upgrade System

## Core rules

* progression is organized by buildings
* **Command Center** controls major progression and building unlocks
* each building has tiers
* unlocking a tier gives access to that tier’s upgrades
* **no prerequisites inside a tier**
* player may buy upgrades in any order within an unlocked tier

## Asset unlock rule

**Unlocking a building tier automatically grants any asset unlock tied to that tier.**
All other upgrades in that tier must still be purchased.

## Tier 1 rule

When a building is unlocked, Tier 1 includes:

* the building unlock itself
* the first asset unlock
* the baseline storage or support tied to that building

# Current Design Principles

* money is the between-mission progression currency
* resources are bought between missions
* aircraft are expensive operational power
* vehicles trade fuel for sustained ground control
* crews use food / fed-status rather than durability
* food represents readiness and operator strain, not just meals
* crew-type assets use **Food wear**, not normal in-mission spending
* durability is a longer-term machine limitation
* tier unlocks automatically grant the asset tied to that tier
* upgrades inside unlocked tiers are bought individually
